## 8. DESIGN SUGGESTIONS

**Layout**
- One-page scrolling site with a sticky top navigation (Home · About · Skills · Projects · Experience · Contact).
- Section order: **Hero → About → Skills → Projects → Experience → Contact.** Projects before Experience — as a student, your projects are your strongest proof.
- Lots of white space. One idea per section. Cards for projects and skills.

**Colors** (pick ONE accent, keep everything else neutral)
- Background: white `#FFFFFF` / light gray `#F7F8FA`
- Text: near-black `#1A1A2E` or dark navy `#0F172A`
- Accent (choose one): professional blue `#2563EB` · teal `#0D9488` · deep navy `#1E3A8A`
- Use the accent only for buttons, links, icons, and section headings.

**Fonts** (max two)
- Headings: **Poppins** or **Montserrat** (semi-bold)
- Body: **Inter** or **Open Sans** (regular)
- All available free on Google Fonts and built into Canva/Wix/Framer.

**Images & icons**
- Hero: a clean, well-lit headshot (smiling, plain background, business-casual). If no headshot yet, use a simple initials monogram ("JM") in your accent color.
- Projects: screenshots of the actual work (Canva designs, Excel dashboard, email workflow diagram). Real screenshots beat stock photos every time.
- Icons: one consistent set — Lucide, Feather, or Canva's outline icons. Line icons in your accent color.
- Avoid: generic stock photos of handshakes, robots, or people pointing at whiteboards.

**Modern & professional checklist**
- Consistent spacing between sections (pick one gap size, use it everywhere).
- Buttons with slightly rounded corners (8px), accent background, white text.
- Subtle card shadows — barely visible, not dramatic.
- Mobile check: preview on your phone before sharing. Most recruiters will open it there first.
